THE AGENTS

Six agents, one go-to-market loop.

Each agent owns a stage of the funnel and does the repetitive 80% of the work — research, enrichment, drafting, tracking. Every card carries its autonomy tier (how far it can act alone) and its build wave (when it ships). Tap a card to open it.

tier autonomy — how far an agent acts without a human tier 4 human-only — money, contracts, pricing wave build order — the release the agent ships in
HOW IT WORKS

One deal, seven stages.

A bank enters as a raw lead and leaves as a signed customer handed to onboarding. Click any stage to see who owns it and what happens. Notice where the funnel turns dark: Deal-Desk escalates to tier 4, and only a human signs contracts and pricing.

Stage 1 / 7

Money & contract close = tier-4, human-only. Agents assemble every artefact — the scored pipeline, the proposal, the pricing config, the draft contract — but a human reviews and signs. No agent can commit Nova to a price or a legal obligation.

HOW IT CONNECTS

Wired into the fleet.

Sales & Growth does not sit alone. It takes qualified demand from Marketing, and when a deal is signed it triggers the machine that actually builds the bank — while every touch keeps the CRM as the single source of truth.

Upstream · Marketing Marketing

Runs campaigns and content, then hands qualified leads down to Sales & Growth — the top of this funnel.

This department Sales & Growth

Finds the right banks and closes them across seven stages. On signature, it fires the trigger that starts provisioning.

Downstream · Provisioning Provisioning pipeline

The new-customer trigger spins up the new bank's repos, infra and CS onboarding.

A signed deal fires new-customer

The instant a human signs, the Deal-Desk emits the new-customer trigger. That kicks off the provisioning pipeline — the same golden path that builds every Nova bank — and formally hands the account to Customer Success onboarding.

Everything feeds Nova Cortex CRM

Every lead, score, demo, proposal and pricing decision is written to Nova Cortex — the CRM system of record. Forecasts, stall alerts and hand-offs all read from one shared truth, so no context is lost between agents or to the humans supervising them.
