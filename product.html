THE AGENTS

Four agents, one design phase

The Product Office is staffed entirely by agents. Each carries an autonomy tier — how much it may do on its own — and a build wave — when it comes online. Every agent here runs at tier 2 (proposes, a human approves) and lands in wave 2. Tap a card to see what it produces.

TIER 2 Proposes — a human approves before anything binds WAVE 2 Second cohort of agents to come online
HOW IT WORKS · THE DESIGN PHASE

From need to design-gated dossier

The DESIGN phase is a relay. A need enters as raw intake and leaves as a Feature Dossier that Engineering can build without guessing. Step through it — the design-gate is where a human decides it is ready.

The output is a Feature Dossier

Not a ticket, not a chat log — a single, coherent unit of work. The dossier bundles the PRD, the architecture and the api-contracts into one design-gated package. Once approved it becomes the unit of work that Engineering picks up, decomposes and delivers.

HOW IT CONNECTS

The front of the SDLC

The Product Office sits at the very front of the software delivery lifecycle. Nothing downstream starts until its work is done and gated. When the human approves the dossier, one event — spec-approved — sets the rest of the machine in motion.

nova-product · SYSTEM OF RECORD The product backlog

Every dossier also feeds nova-product — the durable backlog and system of record for what Nova has decided to build, in priority order, with its rationale attached.

nova-product prioritised backlog

Agents propose, humans approve

The design-gate is a human gate, on purpose. Agents can draft the PRD, the architecture and the contracts in minutes — but a person decides whether the design is right before it becomes committed work. It is the one deliberate handbrake between the Product Office and everything it sets in motion.
