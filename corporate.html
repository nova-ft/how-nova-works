How it works

A department that runs sideways.

Marketing, Sales, Engineering and the rest run down their own lane. Corporate runs across all of them — the horizontal layer every vertical leans on. Legal papers the deals. People onboard the humans. And the Knowledge / Corpus Agent curates the memory the whole fleet shares.

Corporate is cross-cutting, not a silo. It has no customer of its own and no product to ship. Its customer is the rest of the company — the seven other departments and the ~40 agents inside them. That is why it sits at the centre of the diagram below rather than in a lane of its own.

The flywheel

Every department's agents finish work and write their learnings back to the shared corpus. The Knowledge / Corpus Agent curates it — deduping, tagging, flagging stale memory — then serves it back out as reusable skills and context. More work makes the corpus richer; a richer corpus makes every future agent sharper. That compounding loop is the flywheel.

Legal gates the contracts Drafting is autonomous, but a contract does not close until a named human signs. Contract close is a tier-4 checkpoint — the Legal Agent hands a clean, risk-flagged draft to a person and waits.
People handles the humans The HR Agent runs contractor and employee onboarding, right-to-work checks and the paperwork — but equity and options grants are tier-4 and only ever confirmed by a human.
How it connects

Wired into everything.

Corporate touches every other department. Here is where the wires actually run — and the one rule that never bends.

Corpus underpins every memory

No agent has a private brain. Every agent's memory reads from the same two-layer model the Corpus Agent curates — so a lesson learned in Engineering is available to Sales the next morning.

Layer 1 Skills & know-how
Reusable, customer-agnostic learnings: patterns, house-style, playbooks, error dictionaries. Written back by every department, shared by all.
Layer 2 Customer context
Per-bank facts and history, isolated to that customer. Skills apply across everyone; customer context never leaks between banks.

Legal supports Sales

When Sales & Growth reaches terms, the Legal Agent drafts the contract, IP-assignment and NDAs, flags risky terms, and tracks the paper to signature. Sales moves fast; Legal keeps it sound.

It also supports Compliance — generating and reviewing the DPAs that sit under every data-processing arrangement, so the ISO 42001 / GDPR story holds up.

People runs the roster

Whether Nova FT hires a contractor or an employee, the HR Agent runs onboarding, right-to-work, and equity/options admin end to end — so headcount grows without the founders becoming a bottleneck.

Everything it touches feeds the corpus too: a smoother onboarding this quarter is a reusable playbook the next.

The rule that never bends

Agents propose, humans approve. Corporate carries the two decisions Nova FT will never automate: contract close and equity. Both are tier-4, human-only. The agent does the 80% — the draft, the checks, the tracking — and stops at the line.

Agents propose. Humans approve.

For the highest-stakes work, autonomy tops out at proposal. Here is the tier-4 path a contract or an equity grant actually walks:

Tier 2–3
Agent drafts
Legal / HR Agent prepares the contract or grant, pulling clauses and context from the corpus.
Tier 3
Agent flags & tracks
Risky terms surfaced, versions tracked, everything staged for a clean human decision.
Tier 4 · human only
Human signs
A named person closes the contract or confirms the equity. No agent ever crosses this line.
