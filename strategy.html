The fleet

Six agents that steer the business.

Each agent owns a slice of running the company — from market strategy to the invoice. Open a card to see what it does, how far it is trusted to act, and when it gets built. Every action above “explain” is a proposal a human signs off.

Autonomy tier

0 Observe 1 Explain 2 Propose · human approves 3 Act · allowlisted 4 Human-only

Tier 1 · Explain Wave 3 · Later

Reads the market so leadership doesn't have to start from a blank page. It watches competitors, sizes opportunities and frames the strategic choices — always as analysis a human interprets, never as a decision it makes alone.

What it does
  • Continuous market and competitor scans across Nova's regions
  • Positioning options and differentiators, with the evidence behind each
  • Scenario models — best / base / downside — for major bets
  • Briefs that turn signal into a shortlist of strategic choices
Why tier 1. Strategy shapes direction for the whole fleet, so it stays at explain-only — it informs and frames, humans decide.
Tier 2 · Propose Wave 3 · Later

Turns the chosen strategy into a plan the fleet can execute: objectives, measurable key results, a roadmap and the resourcing to make it real. It drafts; leadership commits.

What it does
  • Translates strategic choices into a coherent OKR tree
  • Sequences the roadmap and flags dependencies and conflicts
  • Proposes resourcing — where people and budget should go
  • Tracks progress against key results and surfaces drift early
Why tier 2. It proposes OKRs, roadmaps and resourcing as drafts; a human approves before anything becomes the plan of record.
Tier 2 · Propose Wave 3 · Later

Keeps the board and investors current with one consistent, evidenced story — drawn from the same metrics the business runs on, so the narrative and the numbers never diverge.

What it does
  • Assembles board packs from live BizOps metrics and finance data
  • Drafts monthly / quarterly investor updates in a consistent voice
  • Maintains an always-ready data-room for diligence
  • Answers investor questions with sourced, traceable figures
Why tier 2. Every pack and update is prepared for a human owner to review, edit and sign before it leaves the building.
Tier 1 · Explain Wave 2 · Next

The single source of truth for how the company is doing. It maintains the three headline metrics everyone steers by, keeps the unit economics honest, and runs the models the rest of the fleet reads from.

What it does
  • Owns and publishes the 3 headline metrics — one shared scoreboard
  • Tracks unit economics: cost-to-serve, contribution, payback
  • Maintains the operating and growth models leadership plans against
  • Flags anomalies and explains the “why” behind every movement
Why tier 1. Metrics must be trusted, not edited — it observes and explains only, so the numbers stay clean for every other agent.
Tier 1–2 · Explain / Propose Wave 2 · Next

Knows exactly what each customer is entitled to and what each customer's stack is built from — Nova IP plus every third-party licence — so entitlements and billing always match reality.

What it does
  • Maintains entitlements: what each customer can use, on what plan
  • Tracks third-party licences and their terms, limits and renewals
  • Registers Nova's own IP and where it is deployed
  • Builds a per-customer stack profile that feeds metering and billing
Why tier 1–2. It explains the entitlement picture continuously, and proposes changes (new grants, licence true-ups) for a human to approve.
Tier 2 · Propose Wave 2 · Next

Runs the money mechanics end to end — metering usage, drafting invoices, recognising revenue and forecasting cash — while the moments that actually move money or close the books stay firmly human-only.

What it does
  • Meters transaction usage per customer from the stack profile
  • Drafts invoices, including pass-through of third-party costs
  • Prepares revenue recognition and cash-flow forecasts
  • Compiles R&D-tax evidence from real engineering activity
Tier 2, with a tier-4 line. It proposes invoices and journals for approval — but sending money and closing the books are human-only (tier 4).
The steering loop

How it works.

Strategy & Management runs as a loop, not a hierarchy. Direction becomes plans, plans become metrics, metrics become the story told to boards and the money collected — and all of it feeds back into the next strategic call. Click a stage to follow it round.

In the wider fleet

How it connects to Nova.

Strategy & Management isn't a back office — it's the steering wheel. It sets the direction every other Nova agent executes, meters what each customer uses, and writes back into the platform's own systems of record.

Direction the fleet executes

Strategy sets the destination; OKRs turn it into targets. Every other Nova department — engineering, product, support, compliance — pulls from the same plan, so the whole fleet rows in one direction.

Metering usage per customer

The Licence & Entitlement and Finance / Billing agents meter transaction usage against each customer's stack profile — turning what customers actually consume into invoices, revenue recognition and cash-flow.

Feeds Nova Cortex

Metrics, entitlements and billing write back into Nova Cortex — its CRM and Finance modules — so the same figures the board sees are the figures the platform runs on.
